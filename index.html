About Us

Pan India – Training for Animal-Based Livelihoods

India stands at a pivotal moment in its agricultural transformation journey. With a median age of just 28 years, our nation holds a demographic advantage. Pan India is committed to harnessing this potential through specialized Recognition of Prior Learning programs that empower rural youth with skills in animal husbandry and allied sectors.

Our intensive training program aims to certify and enhance the skills of 20,000 youth across Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand, and Uttarakhand in animal-based livelihoods. This initiative bridges the critical skill gap in livestock and dairy sectors that contribute significantly to India’s agricultural economy.

Non-Farm Agriculture Livelihoods: Defining the Sector

Non-Farm Agriculture & Allied Livelihoods encompass diverse economic activities beyond traditional crop farming. These include animal husbandry, fisheries, apiculture, mushroom cultivation, and other allied enterprises that provide sustainable income and nutritional security. Below are the key categories we specialize in.

Dairy Farming Systems

Cattle & buffalo management, milk processing, dairy cooperative management, feed production & nutrition.

Poultry Production Systems

Broiler & layer farming, hatchery operations, chick management, poultry health & biosecurity.

Small Ruminant Management

Goat & sheep farming, breeding programs, genetic improvement, pasture & fodder cultivation.

Specialized Livestock Operations

Pig & rabbit farming, stray animal management, sustainable livestock operations.

Aquaculture Systems

Fish farming, hatchery operations, fish marketing, integrated fish-livestock systems.

Apiculture (Beekeeping)

Honey bee colony management, honey processing, bee product marketing, pollination services.

Integrated Agricultural Enterprises

Mushroom cultivation, cooperative enterprises, agri-entrepreneurship, value-added products.

Why Focus on Animal-Based Livelihoods?

1. Economic Significance: Livestock sector contributes ~4.11% to India's GDP and supports 20M+ farmers.
2. Employment Potential: Provides year-round income unlike seasonal crop farming.
3. Nutritional Security: Ensures protein supply & balanced nutrition for India's growing population.
4. Rural Economy Backbone: Stable income source for smallholder farmers & landless workers.
5. Women's Participation: Women-friendly occupation with strong potential for gender empowerment.
6. Climate Resilience: More resilient to climate variations compared to crop-based agriculture.

Current Employment Landscape

The India Employment Report 2024 highlights key challenges in youth employment that our programs directly aim to address:

  • Youth Unemployment Crisis: 23.22% of young job seekers were unemployed in 2023, with only 51.25% of assessed youth found employable with required skills.
  • Gender Disparities: Only 19.1% of young women (15-29 years) are engaged in economic activities compared to 53.5% of young men.
  • Agricultural Workforce Decline: India's agricultural workforce is projected to decrease by 25% by 2030 due to urbanization and an aging farmer population.
  • Skill Gap: Despite agriculture employing nearly 45% of India's workforce, a significant gap exists between traditional knowledge and modern scientific practices.
